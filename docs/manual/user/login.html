Logging In & MFA

How to sign into Aspendora Connect, complete multi-factor authentication, and resolve common login issues.

Accessing the Web App

Your administrator will provide you with a web address (URL) for the Aspendora Connect web app. This is typically something like https://phone.yourcompany.com. Open this URL in any modern web browser — Chrome, Firefox, Edge, or Safari all work well.

Bookmark this address so you can find it quickly. If you do not know the URL, ask your administrator or check your company's internal documentation.

Signing In with Email and Password

Follow these steps to log into Aspendora Connect:

  1. Open the Aspendora Connect web app in your browser.
  2. On the login screen, enter your email address in the first field.
  3. Enter your password in the second field.
  4. Click the Sign In button.

If your account does not have multi-factor authentication (MFA) enabled, you will be taken directly to the dashboard. If MFA is enabled, you will see a second screen asking for a verification code — continue to the next section.

Tip
Your email address is not case-sensitive. jane@example.com and Jane@Example.com are treated as the same account.

Completing the MFA Challenge

If your organization requires multi-factor authentication, you will see a verification screen after entering your email and password. This adds an extra layer of security to your account.

  1. Open your authenticator app on your phone (Google Authenticator, Authy, or Microsoft Authenticator).
  2. Find the entry for Aspendora Connect in the app.
  3. Enter the 6-digit code shown in the app into the verification field.
  4. Click Verify.

The code changes every 30 seconds. If you enter the code and it does not work, wait for a new code to appear and try again. Make sure the time on your phone is accurate, as authenticator apps depend on your device clock being correct.

Code Timing
If the 6-digit code in your authenticator app is about to expire (the timer is almost at zero), wait for the next code. Entering an expiring code may cause a verification failure.

Setting Up MFA for the First Time

If your administrator has enabled MFA for your account but you have not set it up yet, you will be guided through the setup process the next time you log in. Here is what to expect:

  1. After entering your email and password, you will see a QR code on screen.
  2. Open your authenticator app and tap the option to add a new account (usually a + icon).
  3. Choose Scan QR code and point your phone's camera at the code on screen.
  4. The app will add a new entry called Aspendora Connect with your email address.
  5. Enter the 6-digit code from the app to confirm setup.
  6. You may also be shown recovery codes. Save these in a safe place — they can be used to log in if you lose access to your authenticator app.

Supported Authenticator Apps

Any TOTP-compatible authenticator app will work. Popular options include:

App Available On Notes
Google Authenticator iOS, Android Free. Simple and widely used.
Microsoft Authenticator iOS, Android Free. Also supports push notifications for Microsoft accounts.
Authy iOS, Android, Desktop Free. Supports cloud backup of your codes across devices.
1Password iOS, Android, Desktop Paid. Stores TOTP codes alongside your passwords.

Staying Logged In

After you sign in, your session stays active for a period of time so you do not have to log in every time you open the app. Here is how it works:

  • Your login session is valid as long as you use the app regularly.
  • If you close the browser and come back later, you may still be logged in.
  • After an extended period of inactivity, you will be asked to sign in again.
  • Your administrator controls the exact session duration — this varies by organization.

To log out manually, click your profile icon in the top-right corner and select Sign Out. This is recommended when using a shared or public computer.

Tip
Always sign out when using a computer that other people have access to. This prevents others from accessing your phone system account.

Forgot Your Password

If you cannot remember your password, you have two options:

  1. Use the password reset link — On the login screen, click Forgot Password. Enter your email address, and a reset link will be sent to your inbox. Click the link and create a new password.
  2. Contact your administrator — If the reset link does not arrive, or if your account is locked, ask your admin to reset your password manually.

When creating a new password, it must be at least 8 characters long. Use a mix of letters, numbers, and symbols for better security. Avoid reusing passwords from other accounts.

SSO Login Coming Soon

In a future release, Aspendora Connect will support Single Sign-On (SSO) with Microsoft and Google. This means you will be able to log in using the same credentials you use for your company email and other corporate applications.

  • Microsoft Entra ID — Sign in with your Microsoft 365 / Azure AD account.
  • Google Workspace — Sign in with your Google company account.

When SSO becomes available, your administrator will configure it for your organization. You will see a "Sign in with Microsoft" or "Sign in with Google" button on the login page.

Troubleshooting Login Issues

If you are having trouble signing in, check the following:

Wrong Password

  • Make sure Caps Lock is off.
  • Double-check that you are typing the correct email address.
  • Try the Forgot Password link to reset it.
  • If you recently changed your password, make sure you are using the new one.

MFA Code Not Working

  • Make sure you are reading the code for Aspendora Connect, not a different account in your authenticator app.
  • Wait for a fresh code if the current one is about to expire.
  • Check that the time on your phone is accurate. Go to Settings > Date & Time and enable automatic time sync.
  • If you changed phones, you may need to set up MFA again — contact your administrator.

Account Locked Out

  • After several failed login attempts, your account may be temporarily locked for security.
  • Wait a few minutes and try again, or contact your administrator to unlock your account.
  • Do not keep trying different passwords while locked out — this will not help and may extend the lockout.

Browser Issues

  • Clear your browser cache and cookies, then try again.
  • Try a different browser or an incognito/private window.
  • Make sure JavaScript is enabled in your browser settings.
  • Check that you are using the correct URL for the Aspendora Connect web app.
Still stuck?
If none of the above solutions work, contact your system administrator. They can check your account status, reset your password, and review login logs to identify the issue.
