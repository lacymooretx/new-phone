Your Extension

View your extension number, manage Do Not Disturb, call forwarding, and other settings you control day to day.

What Is Your Extension Number?

Your extension number is a short number (typically 3 or 4 digits) that identifies you within the phone system. Coworkers can dial your extension to reach you directly without going through the main company number. For example, if your extension is 150, anyone inside your organization can dial 150 to call your desk.

Your extension number is assigned by your administrator. If you need a different extension number, contact your admin to request the change.

Viewing Your Extension Settings

To see your current extension settings:

  1. Log into the Aspendora Connect web app.
  2. Click Your Extension in the left sidebar, or navigate to your profile area.
  3. You will see a summary page showing your extension number, current status, and all configurable options.

The settings page is divided into two sections: settings you can change yourself, and read-only information that only your administrator can modify.

Features You Can Control

The following settings are available for you to change at any time. Adjustments take effect immediately — there is no need to save or restart anything.

Do Not Disturb (DND)

When Do Not Disturb is turned on, incoming calls to your extension will not ring your phone. Instead, callers are sent directly to your voicemail (if enabled) or hear a busy signal.

To toggle Do Not Disturb:

  1. Go to your extension settings page.
  2. Find the Do Not Disturb toggle.
  3. Click the toggle to turn DND on or off.
Tip
Turn on Do Not Disturb when you are in a meeting, on lunch, or need uninterrupted focus time. Remember to turn it off when you are available again, or you will miss calls.
Important
Do Not Disturb affects all your registered devices — your desk phone, the web softphone, and any mobile app. When DND is on, nothing rings.

Call Forwarding

Call forwarding sends your incoming calls to a different number. This is useful when you are working remotely, traveling, or want calls to ring your mobile phone instead of your desk.

To set up call forwarding:

  1. Go to your extension settings page.
  2. Find the Call Forwarding section.
  3. Enter the phone number you want calls forwarded to. This can be an external number (like your cell phone) or another internal extension.
  4. Choose when forwarding should activate:
    • Always — All calls are immediately forwarded.
    • On busy — Calls are forwarded only when you are already on a call.
    • On no answer — Calls are forwarded if you do not pick up within a set number of rings.
  5. Click Save to apply your changes.

To disable call forwarding, clear the forwarding number and save.

Tip
If you forward to an external number (like a cell phone), make sure you include the full number with area code. Your administrator may restrict forwarding to external numbers based on your calling permissions.

Call Waiting

Call waiting controls what happens when a second call comes in while you are already on a call. When enabled, you will hear a beep notifying you of the incoming call, and you can choose to put the current call on hold and answer the new one.

To toggle call waiting:

  1. Go to your extension settings page.
  2. Find the Call Waiting toggle.
  3. Click the toggle to turn it on or off.

When call waiting is off, callers who try to reach you while you are on another call will hear a busy signal or be sent to your voicemail.

Voicemail

Your voicemail settings are managed on a dedicated page. From the extension settings page, click the Manage Voicemail link to jump to your Voicemail settings, where you can change your greeting, PIN, and notification preferences.

Settings Only Your Admin Can Change

The following settings are visible on your extension page for your reference, but only your administrator can modify them. If you need any of these changed, contact your admin.

Setting What It Means
Extension Number Your internal dialing number (e.g., 150). Assigned by your admin.
Outbound Caller ID The phone number that external parties see when you call them. Typically set to your company's main number or a direct line.
Class of Service Defines your calling permissions — whether you can make local, long-distance, or international calls.
Call Recording Whether your calls are recorded. Recording policies are set at the organizational level.
E911 Location The physical address associated with your extension for emergency services. Must be kept accurate by your admin.
Ring Groups & Queues Which ring groups and call queues your extension belongs to. Managed by your admin.

Understanding Your Caller ID

Your caller ID may appear differently depending on who you are calling:

  • Internal calls (to other extensions) — The recipient sees your name and extension number.
  • External calls (to outside numbers) — The recipient sees the outbound caller ID number configured by your administrator. This is typically your company's main phone number or a direct inward dial (DID) number assigned to you.

You cannot change your outbound caller ID yourself. If the wrong number is showing up when you make external calls, let your administrator know.

Device Registration Status

Your extension settings page shows how many devices are currently registered (connected) to your extension. A device is anything that can ring when someone calls you:

  • Desk phone — A physical IP phone on your desk.
  • Web softphone — The browser-based phone in the Aspendora Connect web app.
  • Mobile app — The Aspendora Connect app on your smartphone (when available).

When someone dials your extension, all registered devices ring simultaneously by default. If you see an unexpected number of registrations, or if a device you expect to ring is not ringing, contact your administrator for help.

Tip
If your desk phone shows "No Service" or is not registered, try unplugging the network cable and plugging it back in. If that does not resolve it, your administrator can check the phone's provisioning status remotely.
