Dashboard

Your home screen — a quick overview of your calls, voicemail, and activity for the day.

What the Dashboard Shows You

The dashboard is the first screen you see after logging in. It gives you a summary of your phone activity so you can quickly see what needs your attention without navigating through multiple pages.

The dashboard is personalized to your extension. You only see your own call data, voicemail, and activity. Administrators see a different, more detailed dashboard with system-wide information.

Your Call Activity

The call activity section at the top of the dashboard gives you a snapshot of your phone usage for the current day.

Card What It Shows
Calls Today The total number of calls you have made and received since midnight. This includes both internal extension-to-extension calls and external calls.
Inbound Calls The number of incoming calls you answered today. This helps you track how many calls you have handled.
Outbound Calls The number of calls you initiated today, including calls to outside numbers and other extensions.
Missed Calls Calls that rang your extension but were not answered. These may have gone to voicemail or been picked up by someone else in your ring group. A red badge appears if you have missed calls that you have not reviewed.

Call counts reset at midnight each day. To see calls from previous days, visit the Call History page.

Your Voicemail Summary

The voicemail card shows you how many new (unheard) voicemail messages are waiting in your mailbox. The count is prominently displayed so you can immediately see if someone has left you a message.

  • New messages — Messages you have not listened to yet. Displayed with a notification badge.
  • Total messages — The total number of messages in your voicemail box, including those you have already heard.

Click the voicemail card to jump directly to your Voicemail inbox where you can listen to, manage, and delete messages.

Voicemail Notifications
When a new voicemail arrives, the dashboard updates automatically. You do not need to refresh the page. If you have email notifications enabled, you will also receive an email with the voicemail details.

Recent Calls List

Below the summary cards, the dashboard displays your most recent calls in a compact list. Each entry shows:

  • The caller or recipient name and number
  • Whether the call was inbound, outbound, or missed (indicated by an icon and color)
  • The time the call occurred
  • The call duration

This list shows the last 10 calls. For a complete history with filtering and search, click View All to go to the Call History page.

Quick Actions

The dashboard provides shortcuts so you can take action without navigating to other pages:

  • Call back — Click the phone icon next to any recent call to immediately call that number back. The softphone will open and begin dialing.
  • Check voicemail — Click the voicemail card to jump straight to your inbox and listen to messages.
  • Open softphone — Click the phone icon in the toolbar or press Cmd/Ctrl + Shift + C to open the softphone and make a new call.
  • View missed calls — Click the missed calls card to see a filtered list of calls you did not answer.

Understanding Status Indicators

The dashboard uses colors and icons to help you quickly understand the state of things at a glance.

Indicator Meaning
Green badge Normal status. Everything is working as expected.
Red badge with number Items need your attention. The number indicates how many (missed calls, new voicemails, etc.).
Blue arrow (outbound) A call you made to someone else.
Green arrow (inbound) A call that came in to your extension and was answered.
Red arrow (missed) A call that came in but was not answered by you.

Softphone Connection Status

If the web softphone is enabled for your account, the dashboard header also shows your softphone registration status. This tells you whether you are ready to make and receive calls through the browser.

  • Connected (green dot) — The softphone is registered and ready.
  • Connecting (yellow dot) — The softphone is establishing a connection.
  • Disconnected (red dot) — The softphone is not connected. See the softphone troubleshooting guide for help.

Refreshing Data

The dashboard updates automatically in real time. When a new call comes in or a voicemail arrives, the counts and lists refresh without any action on your part.

If you want to force a manual refresh, you can:

  • Click the Refresh button in the top-right corner of the dashboard area.
  • Refresh your browser page (Cmd/Ctrl + R). This reloads the entire application.
Data Freshness
Call counts and voicemail totals are updated in real time via a live connection to the server. If the real-time connection drops (for example, due to a brief network interruption), the dashboard will reconnect automatically. A small banner at the top will notify you if the connection is temporarily lost.

Dashboard Time Range

By default, the dashboard shows data for today (since midnight in your local time zone). The time zone is determined by your browser settings. All times displayed on the dashboard use your local time.

For historical data beyond today, use the Call History page, which allows you to search and filter across any date range.
