Getting Started

Welcome to Aspendora Connect. This guide covers everything you need to know as an everyday user of the phone system.

Welcome to Aspendora Connect

Aspendora Connect is your company's business phone system. It connects your desk phone, your computer, and your mobile device so you can make and receive calls from anywhere. Your administrator has already set up the system for you — this guide will help you get the most out of it.

Whether you are sitting at your desk, working from home, or traveling, Aspendora Connect keeps you connected with your team and your customers through a single phone number and a unified experience across all your devices.

What You Can Do

As a Aspendora Connect user, you have access to the following features from the web interface:

  • Make and receive calls — Use the built-in web softphone directly from your browser, or pick up your desk phone as usual.
  • Check voicemail — Listen to messages, manage folders, and get email notifications when new voicemails arrive.
  • View call history — See a complete log of your incoming, outgoing, and missed calls with times and durations.
  • Use the web softphone — Make calls straight from your computer without needing a physical phone.
  • Manage your settings — Toggle Do Not Disturb, set up call forwarding, and update your voicemail greeting.
  • See your dashboard — Get a quick overview of missed calls, new voicemails, and your recent activity.

Quick Start

Follow these four steps to get up and running right away:

Step 1 — Log In
Open the web address your administrator gave you and sign in with your email and password. If your organization uses multi-factor authentication (MFA), you will also enter a code from your authenticator app. See Logging In for detailed instructions.
Step 2 — Check Your Extension
After logging in, visit Your Extension to see your extension number and confirm your settings. This is the number your coworkers dial to reach you internally.
Step 3 — Set Up Voicemail
Go to Voicemail to set your PIN and record a personal greeting. Callers who reach your voicemail will hear this greeting before leaving a message.
Step 4 — Make a Test Call
Try calling a coworker to make sure everything works. You can use your desk phone or the Web Softphone built into the web app. Dial their extension number to place an internal call.

Your Role vs. Your Admin's Role

Aspendora Connect has two types of users: administrators and regular users. As a regular user, you control your own day-to-day settings like Do Not Disturb, call forwarding, and voicemail. Your administrator handles the system-level configuration that keeps everything running smoothly.

You Control Your Admin Controls
Do Not Disturb on/off Extension numbers and assignments
Call forwarding destinations Caller ID and outbound routing
Voicemail greeting and PIN Call recording policies
Call waiting on/off Ring groups and call queues
Personal profile settings Phone provisioning and E911 locations

If you need a change that only an administrator can make — such as a new extension number or updated caller ID — contact your admin using the information below.

Getting Help

If you run into a problem or have a question that this guide does not answer, reach out to your company's phone system administrator. They can help with:

  • Account lockouts and password resets
  • Extension changes or new phone setup
  • Permission changes (e.g., international calling)
  • Troubleshooting call quality or connectivity issues
  • Any feature requests or questions about what the system can do
Tip
Your admin may have provided a support email, a help desk ticket system, or a chat channel for phone system requests. Check your company's internal documentation or ask your manager for the best way to get help.
