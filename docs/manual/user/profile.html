Your Profile

View and update your personal information, change your password, and manage your security settings.

Accessing Your Profile

To open your profile settings, click on your name or avatar in the top-right corner of the application and select Profile from the dropdown menu. You can also navigate there using the keyboard shortcut G then P.

The profile page shows your current account information and provides forms to update the settings you are allowed to change.

Viewing Your Profile

The top section of the profile page displays your current account details in a read-only summary:

Field Description
Full name Your first and last name as it appears in the system. This name is shown in the directory, caller ID for internal calls, and voicemail greetings.
Email address Your email used for login and notifications. Voicemail-to-email and other alerts are sent here.
Extension Your assigned phone extension number (read-only). Set by your administrator.
Role Your permission level in the system (read-only). Common roles include User, Operator, and Manager.
Organization The tenant (company) your account belongs to (read-only).
Last login The date and time of your most recent login to the application.

Changing Your Name

Your name appears in the company directory and internal caller ID.

  1. Find the Name section and click Edit.
  2. Update your first name, last name, or both.
  3. Click Save to apply the changes.

The change takes effect immediately across the application.

Name Display
Your name is used for internal caller ID. External callers see the caller ID configured on your outbound route, which is controlled by your administrator.

Changing Your Email

Your email address is used for logging in and receiving notifications such as voicemail-to-email alerts and missed call summaries.

  1. On the profile page, find the Email section.
  2. Click Edit next to your email address.
  3. Enter your new email address.
  4. Enter your current password to confirm the change.
  5. Click Save.
Email Change Affects Login
After changing your email, you must use the new email address to log in. Your old email address will no longer work for authentication. Make sure you have access to the new email address before making this change.

Changing Your Password

You should change your password periodically and any time you suspect it may have been compromised. To change your password:

  1. On the profile page, find the Password section.
  2. Click Change Password.
  3. Enter your current password in the first field. This confirms your identity.
  4. Enter your new password in the second field.
  5. Enter the new password again in the confirm password field to make sure you typed it correctly.
  6. Click Save.

Password Requirements

Your new password must meet these requirements:

  • At least 8 characters long
  • No more than 128 characters long
  • Cannot be the same as your current password

For the strongest security, use a unique password that you do not use on other websites. Consider using a password manager to generate and store strong passwords.

Sessions After Password Change
When you change your password, all of your other active sessions (other browsers, devices) will be signed out. You will need to log in again on those devices using your new password.

Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA)

Multi-factor authentication adds an extra layer of security to your account by requiring a code from an authenticator app in addition to your password when you log in.

Enabling MFA

  1. In the Security section, click Enable MFA.
  2. Scan the QR code with your authenticator app (Google Authenticator, Microsoft Authenticator, or Authy).
  3. Enter the 6-digit code from the app and click Verify and Enable.

Once enabled, you will need a code from your authenticator app every time you log in. See the Logging In page for the MFA login flow.

Disabling MFA

  1. In the Security section, click Disable MFA.
  2. Enter your current password and a valid authenticator code to confirm.
MFA May Be Required
Your administrator may require MFA for all accounts. If MFA is mandatory for your organization, you will not see the option to disable it. Contact your administrator if you have questions about MFA requirements.

Lost Your Authenticator Device?

If you lose access to your authenticator app, contact your administrator to have MFA reset so you can log in and set it up again with your new device.

What You Cannot Change

Some account settings are controlled by your administrator and cannot be modified from your profile page. These include:

Setting Why It Is Locked Who Can Change It
Role Your permission level determines what you can access in the system. Changing roles requires administrative authorization. Your administrator
Organization / Tenant Your account is tied to a specific company (tenant). Moving accounts between tenants is an administrative operation. MSP administrator
Extension number Extension assignments are managed by administrators to ensure proper call routing and avoid conflicts. Your administrator
Caller ID (external) The caller ID shown on outbound calls is configured at the route level and tied to your phone numbers. Your administrator

If you need any of these changed, contact your administrator or IT support team.

Last Login Information

The bottom of your profile page shows your most recent login date, time, and method. This helps you verify that no one else has accessed your account.

Suspicious Activity
If you see unfamiliar login activity, change your password immediately, enable MFA if it is not already on, and notify your administrator.
