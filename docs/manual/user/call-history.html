Call History

View your recent calls, understand call types and results, filter your log, and listen to recordings.

Viewing Your Recent Calls

Your call history shows a complete record of every call involving your extension. To view it:

  1. Log into the New Phone web app.
  2. Click Call History in the left sidebar.
  3. Your most recent calls appear at the top of the list.

The call history page loads your most recent calls by default. You can scroll down to load older calls, or use the date filter to jump to a specific time period.

Understanding the Call Log

Each entry in your call log shows the following information:

Column What It Shows
Direction Whether the call was inbound, outbound, or internal (see below).
Caller / From The person or number that placed the call. For inbound calls, this is who called you. For outbound calls, this shows your extension.
Called / To The person or number that was dialed. For inbound calls, this is your extension. For outbound calls, this is who you called.
Date & Time When the call started, shown in your local time zone.
Duration How long the call lasted (talk time). Missed calls and unanswered calls show 0:00.
Result How the call ended — answered, missed, busy, or voicemail (see below).
Recording A play button appears if the call was recorded and you have permission to listen.

Call Directions

Each call is labeled with a direction indicator so you can quickly see what kind of call it was:

Direction Icon Description
Inbound Arrow pointing down or left A call that came in to your extension from an outside number or the main company line.
Outbound Arrow pointing up or right A call you placed to an outside number.
Internal Double arrow or phone icon A call between two extensions within your organization. This includes calls you made to coworkers and calls coworkers made to you.
Tip
Internal calls are only visible when both extensions are in the same tenant. Calls between different organizations do not appear as internal.

Call Results

The result column tells you how each call ended. This helps you quickly identify missed calls or calls that went to voicemail.

Result What It Means
Answered The call was picked up and a conversation took place. Duration reflects the talk time.
Missed An inbound call that rang but was not answered. The caller may have hung up or been redirected.
Busy The caller received a busy signal because you were on another call and call waiting was off.
Voicemail The caller was sent to your voicemail and may have left a message. Check your Voicemail page.
No Answer An outbound call you placed that was not answered by the other party.
Failed The call could not be completed due to a network issue, invalid number, or permission restriction.
Missed calls need attention
Missed calls are highlighted in the call log so they stand out. If you see missed calls, consider calling back the person or checking if they left a voicemail. Your dashboard also shows a count of missed calls since your last login.

Filtering Your Call History

When you need to find a specific call or narrow down the list, use the filter controls at the top of the call history page:

  • By date — Click the Date filter and pick a preset (Today, Last 7 Days, Last 30 Days) or a custom range.
  • By direction — Click the Direction filter and choose Inbound, Outbound, or Internal.
  • By result — Click the Result filter and choose Answered, Missed, Voicemail, etc.

You can combine multiple filters. For example, set direction to Inbound and result to Missed to see only incoming calls you did not answer. Active filters appear as tags below the filter bar — click the X on any tag to remove it.

Listening to Call Recordings

If your organization records calls and you have permission to access recordings, you will see a play button in the Recording column for recorded calls. To listen:

  1. Find the call with a recording (indicated by a play icon).
  2. Click the Play button.
  3. An audio player will appear. Use the controls to play, pause, rewind, or fast-forward.

Not all calls are recorded. Whether calls are recorded depends on your organization's recording policy, which is configured by your administrator. If you need access to a recording but do not see a play button, contact your admin.

Privacy notice
Call recordings may contain sensitive information. Handle them with care and follow your organization's policies regarding call recording access and sharing.

Finding a Specific Call

Use the search box to type a phone number, extension, or contact name. Combine this with date and direction filters to narrow results further. Click any column header to sort by that field.

Exporting Call History

If your administrator has enabled export, click the Export button at the top-right of the page and choose CSV. The file includes direction, caller, called number, date, time, duration, and result. Recordings are not included in the export.

Tip
CSV files open in any spreadsheet application (Excel, Google Sheets, LibreOffice Calc) and are useful for generating call reports or tracking call volume over time.
