Web Softphone

Make and receive phone calls directly from your web browser without any additional software or hardware.

What Is the Web Softphone?

The web softphone is a built-in phone inside the Aspendora Connect web application. It lets you make and receive calls using your computer's microphone and speakers (or a headset) instead of a physical desk phone. Your calls use the same extension number and caller ID as your desk phone, so callers will not notice any difference.

The softphone uses WebRTC technology and works entirely within your browser with nothing to install. As long as you are logged in, you can place and receive calls.

Requirements

Before using the softphone, make sure you have the following:

Requirement Details
Browser Google Chrome, Microsoft Edge, Firefox, or Safari (latest version recommended)
Microphone Built-in or external microphone — you must grant the browser permission to use it
Audio output Speakers or headphones — a headset with microphone is strongly recommended
Network Stable internet connection — wired Ethernet is preferred over Wi-Fi
Permissions Your administrator must have enabled the softphone feature for your extension
Headset Recommended
A headset dramatically improves call quality by reducing echo and background noise. A USB or Bluetooth headset with a noise-cancelling microphone is ideal.

Opening the Softphone

Click the phone icon in the top-right toolbar, or press Cmd/Ctrl + Shift + C. The first time you open it, your browser will ask for microphone permission — click Allow to grant access.

Making a Call

  1. Open the softphone panel.
  2. Type the phone number or extension in the dial pad field (or use the on-screen buttons).
  3. Click the green Call button or press Enter.
  4. You will hear ringing. When the other person picks up, the call timer starts.

For internal calls, type the extension number (e.g. 101). For outside calls, dial the full number with area code (e.g. 5551234567). Your administrator may require a prefix like 9 for outside calls.

Receiving a Call

When someone calls your extension, a notification appears showing the caller's name and number. You will hear a ringtone through your speakers or headset.

  • Answer — Click the green button or press Cmd/Ctrl + Shift + A.
  • Decline — Click the red button to send the call to voicemail.
Browser Tab Must Be Open
The softphone only works while the Aspendora Connect web app is open. If you close the tab, calls follow your normal routing (desk phone, voicemail, etc.).

During a Call

While on an active call, the softphone panel shows several controls:

Button What It Does
Mute Silences your microphone so the other person cannot hear you. Click again to unmute. The button highlights when muted.
Hold Places the caller on hold with music. Click again to resume the call. The caller hears hold music while waiting.
Transfer Sends the call to another extension or phone number. You can do a blind transfer (instant) or an attended transfer (talk first, then transfer).
Keypad Opens the DTMF dial pad so you can press digits during a call. Useful for navigating automated phone menus or entering conference PINs.
Hang Up Ends the call immediately. You can also press Cmd/Ctrl + Shift + H.

Transferring a Call

  • Blind transfer — Click Transfer, enter the destination, click Send. The call goes directly without announcing it.
  • Attended transfer — Click Transfer, enter the destination, click Call First. Speak with them, then click Complete Transfer.

Selecting Audio Devices

If you have multiple microphones or speakers, click the Settings gear icon in the softphone panel to choose which devices to use.

  • Microphone — Select the input device for your voice.
  • Speaker — Select the output device for call audio.
  • Ringtone device — Optionally set a different speaker for the ringtone so you hear incoming calls even when wearing headphones.

Changes take effect immediately. Use the Test buttons to verify each device.

Registration Status

The softphone panel displays a small status indicator showing whether your phone is connected and ready to make or receive calls.

Indicator Meaning
Green dot Connected and registered. You can make and receive calls.
Yellow dot Connecting or re-registering. Calls may not work for a moment.
Red dot Disconnected. You cannot make or receive calls. Check your internet connection and try refreshing the page.

If the status stays red for more than a minute, try refreshing the browser page. If the problem persists, contact your administrator.

Tips for Best Audio Quality

  • Use a headset — Prevents echo and feedback from laptop speakers.
  • Use a wired connection — Ethernet is more reliable than Wi-Fi for voice calls.
  • Close unnecessary tabs — Streaming video or audio in other tabs competes for bandwidth.
  • Check your bandwidth — Voice calls need about 100 kbps in each direction.
  • Stay in a quiet environment — Use a noise-cancelling microphone or find a quiet space.

Troubleshooting

No Audio on Calls

If you or the other party cannot hear anything:

  1. Check that your microphone and speakers are selected in the softphone audio settings.
  2. Make sure the browser has microphone permission (look for an icon in the address bar).
  3. Verify your system volume is up and not muted.
  4. Check that the softphone mute button is not active.
  5. Refresh the page and try the call again.

Cannot Register (Red Status)

If the status indicator stays red:

  1. Check your internet connection by loading another website.
  2. Make sure you are logged in to the Aspendora Connect application.
  3. Refresh the browser page or clear your cache.
  4. If you are behind a firewall or VPN, it may be blocking WebRTC traffic. Contact your administrator.

Echo or One-Way Audio

If you hear echo or only one side can hear the other:

  • Switch to a headset — echo is most common with laptop speakers.
  • Lower your speaker volume to prevent microphone pickup.
  • Try a wired connection instead of Wi-Fi, or disconnect your VPN.
  • Contact your administrator if the problem continues.
