Voicemail

Listen to messages, manage your mailbox folders, set your greeting, and configure notifications.

Checking Your Voicemail

The easiest way to check your voicemail is through the Aspendora Connect web app. After logging in, click Voicemail in the left sidebar to open your mailbox. You will see a list of all your messages with the newest ones at the top.

Each message in the list shows:

  • Caller — The name or number of the person who left the message.
  • Date and time — When the message was left.
  • Duration — How long the message is.
  • Status — Whether the message is new (unread) or has been listened to.
  • Folder — Which folder the message is currently in.
Tip
New (unread) messages are shown in bold. Once you play a message, it is automatically marked as read and moved to the Old folder unless you move it elsewhere.

Listening to Messages

To play a voicemail message:

  1. Open the Voicemail page from the sidebar.
  2. Find the message you want to listen to.
  3. Click the Play button next to the message.
  4. The message will play through your computer speakers or headphones.

You can pause, rewind, or fast-forward using the audio player controls. The player also shows the current playback position and total duration.

If a message has been transcribed, you will also see a text version of the voicemail below the audio player. Transcription accuracy depends on audio quality and the caller's speech clarity.

Message Folders

Your voicemail is organized into folders to help you keep track of your messages. The default folders are:

Folder Purpose
Inbox New messages that you have not listened to yet.
Old Messages you have already listened to. Messages move here automatically after playback.
Work A folder for organizing work-related messages.
Family A folder for personal or family messages.
Friends A folder for messages from friends or non-work contacts.

Use the folder tabs at the top of the voicemail page to switch between folders. The number next to each folder name shows how many messages it contains.

Moving Messages Between Folders

To organize your messages into different folders:

  1. Find the message you want to move.
  2. Click the Move button (or use the dropdown menu next to the message).
  3. Select the destination folder from the list.
  4. The message will be moved immediately.

You can also select multiple messages at once using the checkboxes, then use the bulk action menu to move them all to the same folder.

Marking Messages as Read or Unread

Messages are automatically marked as read when you play them. If you want to mark a message as unread (so it appears bold and shows up in your new message count):

  1. Find the message in your list.
  2. Click the three-dot menu next to the message.
  3. Select Mark as Unread.

This is useful when you listen to a message but want to come back to it later as a reminder.

Forwarding a Message to Another Mailbox

If someone else should hear a voicemail, click Forward on the message, enter the recipient's extension number, add an optional note, and click Send. The recipient sees the forwarded message in their Inbox with your name as the forwarder.

Deleting Messages

Click the Delete button (trash icon) on any message and confirm when prompted. Deletion may be permanent depending on your organization's retention policy. If a message is important, move it to a named folder instead.

Email Notifications

Aspendora Connect can send you an email every time a new voicemail arrives, so you know about messages even when you are not in the web app. Depending on configuration, the email may include just a notification or the audio file as an attachment. If you are not receiving notifications, ask your admin to enable them.

Tip
If voicemail emails go to spam, add the notification sender address to your safe senders list.

Setting Your Greeting

Your voicemail greeting is the message callers hear before they leave a voicemail. If your administrator allows it, you can record a custom greeting:

  1. Go to the Voicemail page in the web app.
  2. Click Settings or the gear icon.
  3. Under Greeting, choose one of the following:
    • Default — Uses the system greeting with your extension number.
    • Name only — Plays your recorded name followed by the system prompt.
    • Custom — Plays a fully custom greeting that you record.
  4. If you select Custom, click Record and speak your greeting. Click Stop when finished.
  5. Play back the recording to make sure it sounds good.
  6. Click Save to apply your new greeting.
Tip for a good greeting
Keep it brief and professional. Include your name and a simple instruction: "Hi, you've reached [Name] at [Company]. I'm unable to take your call right now. Please leave a message and I'll get back to you as soon as possible."

Changing Your Voicemail PIN

Your voicemail PIN is used to access your mailbox from a desk phone. It is separate from your web app password. To change your PIN:

  1. Go to the Voicemail page and click Settings.
  2. Under PIN, enter your new PIN (minimum 4 digits).
  3. Click Save.

Choose a PIN that is easy to remember but hard to guess. Avoid patterns like 1234 or 0000.

Checking Voicemail from Your Desk Phone

You can also check your voicemail directly from your desk phone without using the web app. This is useful when you are at your desk and want to quickly listen to messages.

  1. Pick up your desk phone handset or press the speakerphone button.
  2. Dial *97 (or press the dedicated voicemail button if your phone has one).
  3. Enter your voicemail PIN when prompted, followed by the # key.
  4. Follow the voice prompts to listen to, save, or delete messages:
    • Press 1 to listen to new messages.
    • Press 2 to listen to old messages.
    • Press 0 for mailbox options (greeting, PIN, name recording).
    • Press * to return to the main menu.

During playback: press 1 to replay, 3 to skip, 5 to forward, 7 to delete, 9 to save, * for the main menu, # to exit.

Tip
The message waiting indicator (MWI) light on your desk phone turns on when you have new voicemail. It turns off after you listen to all new messages.
